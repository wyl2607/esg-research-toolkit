# ESG Research Toolkit v0.2.2 Release

**Release Date**: 2026-04-17
**Previous Version**: v0.2.1 (2026-04-16)
**Status**: ✅ Production Ready

## Overview

Data integrity release. Roots out the **company-name identity split** that
was preventing multi-year trend charts from rendering correctly for VW,
SAP, RWE and 5 other companies. Coverage of companies with ≥ 2 years of
history goes from **5 → 8**, and all 7 core DAX companies (BASF, BMW, DHL,
Deutsche Telekom, RWE, SAP, Volkswagen) now have the full 2022–2024 trend.

## Root Cause

`scripts/seed_german_demo.py::upload_company()` POSTed PDFs to
`/report/upload` and used whatever `company_name` the AI extractor returned.
The manifest's curated `company_name` was never used to override this, so
the same legal entity landed under slightly different names per PDF vintage
(e.g. `SAP` from 2022/2023 filings vs `SAP SE` from 2024, `Volkswagen Group`
from 2022/2023 vs `Volkswagen AG` from 2024). The SQLAlchemy unique
constraint is `(company_name, report_year)`, so these became separate
identity rows and multi-year aggregation never joined them.

## Three-Layer Fix

### Layer 1 — Canonicalization (prevents future drift from new sources)

`report_parser/company_identity.py` gains 9 new aliases. `SAP`, `RWE`,
`Volkswagen Group`, `BMW Group`, `Deutsche Telekom`, `Linde`, `PUMA`,
`Fresenius`, `thyssenkrupp` now canonicalize automatically.

### Layer 2 — Upload override (prevents drift even without alias coverage)

`/report/upload` accepts an optional `override_company_name` form field.
When present, it replaces the AI-extracted name unconditionally. The seed
pipeline now passes `manifest.company_name` on every upload, making the
manifest the canonical source of truth.

### Layer 3 — Historical backfill

`scripts/migrate_canonical_company_names.py` rewrites legacy rows to their
canonical names and, when that produces a `(canonical, year)` collision,
keeps the higher-quality record (via `report_quality_score`) and deletes
the rest. 14 rows renamed, 6 duplicates removed.
